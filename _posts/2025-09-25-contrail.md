She pushed off just after dawn, the water still carrying the night. Her calendar weighed on her shoulders with the specter of meetings, obligations, and the sense of being pulled forward into a day already claimed. Then, above the river, she caught it: a crooked chalk-line unraveling across the sky, fresh and raw. 

<!--more-->

Not cloud, not weather. More like the scrawl of a hurried hand.

Then it hit her. She’d just missed a launch. Strange that only human minds could inscribe such a mark, half-scribble, half-signature. What would the ancients have called it? A warning, a blessing, a god’s command?

She turned the kayak homeward. The rocket contrail drifted behind her, dissolving.
